We Breathe Dust.

Living in Delhi in winter feels like being trapped in a gas chamber.

Even with home filters, stepping outside to work—delivering packages, driving cabs, selling on the street—means breathing toxic air. For those who must travel all day, the risk is daily and cumulative.

Oct — Jan: Peak Crisis
Cool air, temperature inversions, stagnant winds and smoke from crop-residue burning combine to spike PM2.5 and PM10 across Delhi.

The hidden cost of every breath

Why the air gets worse in winter

A mix of local emissions and regional smoke, plus stagnant meteorology, drive the crisis.

  • Road & construction dust — major source of PM10 and re-suspended dust.
  • Vehicle & industrial emissions — continuous sources of PM2.5.
  • Seasonal crop-residue burning — regional plumes worsen Delhi’s winter peaks.
  • Meteorology — temperature inversions and low wind trap pollutants near the ground.

Why current “solutions” fall short

Short-term measures help for a while — then the haze returns.

  • Water sprinkling — reduces coarse dust briefly; needs repeated application.
  • Construction bans & odd-even — reactive, disruptive, and not a permanent fix.
  • Dust suppressants — promising, but require safe chemistries, repeat application and monitoring.

Conclusion: No single silver bullet exists today. We need combined, systemic action and scalable innovation.
